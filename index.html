Frequently Asked Questions

Dialysis is a procedure to remove waste products and excess fluid from the blood when the kidneys stop working properly. It often involves diverting blood to a machine to be cleaned.
A typical hemodialysis session lasts about 4 hours, and it is usually done 3 times a week. However, the exact time can vary based on individual medical requirements.
Yes, our facilities are equipped to provide 24/7 emergency dialysis support with critical care monitors and experienced rapid-response teams.
An AV fistula is a connection made by a vascular surgeon between an artery and a vein, specifically for dialysis access. We provide regular monitoring, cleaning, and maintenance to ensure it remains healthy and functional.
